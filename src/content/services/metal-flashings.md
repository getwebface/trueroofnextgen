## 🛑 The "Temporary Sealant" Trap: Are You Masking a Structural Leak?

**The Problem:** You notice a brown water stain on your ceiling near the chimney. A general tradesperson climbs up, spots a crack in the 40-year-old lead flashing, and simply smears a thick layer of industrial silicone over the gap.

> "It's sealed. That should stop the water."

**The Agitation:** ❌ **Wrong.** Silicone is a superficial band-aid over a failing structural component.

Lead flashings crack because they become brittle over decades of extreme temperature fluctuations. Smearing sealant over a crack doesn't restore the metal's integrity. As the house continues to naturally expand and contract, the sealant inevitably splits or peels away from the oxidized metal. Water quietly resumes channeling directly down the brickwork and into your rafters. By the time the ceiling stain reappears, you are dealing with deeply rotted structural timber.

**The Solution:** ✅ **Professional Metal Replacement.**

We don't deal in temporary superficial solutions. If a flashing or valley iron has failed, we remove the compromised metal entirely and install a precision-engineered, high-grade replacement. It is the only way to guarantee the watertight integrity of your home's structural transitions.
