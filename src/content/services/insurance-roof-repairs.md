When a severe Melbourne storm hits, the immediate priority is protecting your home's interior from further water damage. True Roof provides rapid, professional make-safe services and detailed condition reports specifically tailored for insurance claims involving terracotta and concrete tiled roofs.

Storm damage often manifests as dislodged ridge caps, shattered tiles from hail or debris, or torn valley irons. A quick tarp job might stop the immediate drip, but it's not a permanent solution. We focus on securing the site and preparing for a proper restoration.

**The True Roof Make-Safe & Assessment Protocol**

Our approach ensures your property is protected immediately while laying the groundwork for a lasting repair that aligns with our 4-Stage Restoration Process.

<div class="grid grid-cols-1 md:grid-cols-2 gap-4 mt-8 mb-8">

<div class="glass-card" style="padding: 1.5rem;">

**Immediate Tarping & Securing:** We rapidly deploy to secure compromised areas, using heavy-duty tarpaulins and specialized securing methods to stop water ingress without causing additional damage to surrounding healthy tiles.

</div>

<div class="glass-card" style="padding: 1.5rem;">

**Comprehensive Condition Reporting:** Insurance companies require proof. We provide detailed, photographic evidence of the storm damage versus pre-existing wear and tear, highlighting issues like freshly broken tiles, lifted flashings, or compromised flexi-pointing.

</div>

<div class="glass-card" style="padding: 1.5rem;">

**Targeted Structural Repairs:** Once the claim is approved, we move beyond the tarp. We meticulously replace damaged tiles, install new high-sided valley irons if compromised, and repair lead chimney flashings to restore the roof's structural integrity.

</div>

<div class="glass-card" style="padding: 1.5rem;">

**Permanent Re-bedding & Pointing:** Storms often dislodge ridge caps. We don't just patch them; we perform a complete re-bedding and apply modern flexible pointing compounds to ensure they remain secure through future extreme weather events.

</div>
