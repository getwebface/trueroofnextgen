The ridge caps on your roof are the critical highest points where different roof faces meet. Traditionally, these caps were secured using standard sand and cement mortar. While effective initially, this rigid mortar inevitably cracks, crumbles, and fails over time due to the natural expansion, contraction, and structural movement of your home.

When the mortar breaks down, your ridge caps become loose, creating direct entry points for water ingress, wind-driven rain, and even pests, leading to significant ceiling and structural damage.

**The True Roof Re-bedding & Pointing Solution**

As a core component of our 4-Stage Restoration Process, our ridge cap re-bedding and flexi-pointing service ensures a permanent, watertight seal that adapts to your home's movement.

## 🛑 The "Silicone Trap": Is a Quick Patch Destroying Your Ceiling?

**The Problem:** You spot a crack in the mortar along your roof's ridge line. It's just a small gap, so you, or a general handyman, grab a tube of hardware-store silicone to seal it up.

> "It's waterproof, right? A quick patch is all it needs to stop the leak."

**The Agitation:** ❌ **Wrong.** Silicone is a temporary band-aid on a structural failure.

While the silicone might block the surface gap for a few months, the underlying cement bed continues to crumble and fail underneath it. Water inevitably bypasses the patch, pooling inside the ridge line and funneling directly down your rafters. By the time you notice the leak again, you aren't just looking at cracked mortar—you're looking at rotted structural timber and ruined interior plasterboard.

**The Solution:** ✅ **Professional Re-bedding & Flexi-Pointing.**

We don't do quick patches. We completely remove the failing, rigid mortar down to the structural base. We re-bed the ridge caps straight and true using high-strength mortar, then seal them permanently with modern, polymer-based flexi-pointing that moves naturally with your home, preventing cracks before they start.
